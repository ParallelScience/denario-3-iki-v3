The "Job Security Paradox": I hypothesize that the perceived threat to job security is not driven by AI adoption itself, but by a "Task-Autonomy Mismatch" where employees are comfortable with AI performing routine tasks (QGM) but perceive a loss of personal agency (QEA_14) when AI is deployed for high-level decision-making. 

To test this, I propose a "Task-Agency Interaction Analysis":
1. Construct a "Task Autonomy Index" (TAI) by clustering the 17 binary QGM items into "Routine/Execution" vs. "Strategic/Decision-making" tasks.
2. Calculate an "Agency Gap" score by measuring the delta between an employee's comfort with AI-driven execution and their perceived impact on personal agency (QEA_14).
3. Test the hypothesis that the "Anxiously Declining" class identified in previous research is specifically characterized by high comfort in AI-driven routine tasks but a high negative delta in personal agency.
4. Use a moderation analysis to determine if "Employee Involvement" (QKB_1_11) acts as a buffer that specifically mitigates the negative impact of the "Agency Gap" on job security, effectively decoupling the fear of displacement from the reality of task automation.
