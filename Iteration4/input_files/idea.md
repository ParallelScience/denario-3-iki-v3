**Title: Latent Class Trajectories of AI-Induced Job Security: Identifying Organizational Catalysts for Professional Stability**

This study replaces interval-based delta analysis with Latent Class Analysis (LCA) to categorize the 2,603 respondents into distinct psychological trajectory classes (e.g., "Resiliently Optimistic," "Anxiously Declining," and "Stagnant Neutral") based on the joint distribution of current (`QEA_2`) and 3-year expected (`QEB_2`) job security. By applying a multinomial logistic regression, we determine how specific organizational enablers (`QKB`) and cultural attributes (`QGO`)—encoded as composite indices—predict membership in the "Resiliently Optimistic" versus "Anxiously Declining" classes. To address multicollinearity, we condense the 16 binary `QHD` emotional attitude items into a lower-dimensional latent "Affective Disposition" score, which serves as a covariate alongside baseline job profile (`HIDDG`) and sector (`QDB`). The research objective is to isolate the structural configurations—specifically, the interaction between "employee involvement in development" (QKB_1_11) and "regular training" (QKB_1_4)—that statistically increase the probability of an employee occupying a positive stability class. This approach provides a rigorous, non-linear framework for identifying the specific organizational policies that effectively decouple AI-driven task evolution from perceived job displacement.
