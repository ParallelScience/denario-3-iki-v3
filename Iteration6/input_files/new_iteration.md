- Justification: The current project is already at a high level of technical maturity, having successfully transitioned from simple regression to a robust Latent Class Analysis (LCA). The proposed improvements do not represent "unnecessary complexity" but rather address the "So What?" factor by transforming descriptive findings into actionable, causal-leaning insights. Specifically, treating task transformation as a mediating mechanism rather than a mere covariate directly addresses the core research question of how organizational policies translate into professional stability. Furthermore, the "Policy Trap" analysis (the negative interaction between training and accountability) provides a critical, counter-intuitive contribution that significantly increases the "Acceptance Delta" for top-tier management or organizational behavior journals. The ROI of effort is high because the data is already cleaned and the LCA framework is established; these refinements leverage existing work to move the paper from a "solid empirical study" to a "definitive policy-guiding framework."
